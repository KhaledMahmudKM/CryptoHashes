>hash calculations

compute hashes in your browser

An interactive playground for cryptographic hashing. Type into any section to compute message digests, keyed MACs (HMAC), and derived keys live, then compare algorithms side by side. Each section is tagged with how it's computed — by your browser's built-in cryptography or by an external library. Everything runs locally in your browser; nothing you type is sent anywhere.

MD5 & RIPEMD-160

external library

Computed by Paul Johnston's MD5 and Jeremy Lin's RIPEMD-160 implementations (hex_md5, hex_rmd160 and their HMAC variants), loaded as separate scripts. Output is hex only; these libraries don't share the hex/base64 toggle above.

04 / sponge

SHA-3 & Keccak

external library

Computed by js-sha3 (Chen Yi-Cyuan, MIT License), loaded from cdnjs. SHA-3 is the FIPS 202 standard; Keccak is the original submission it was based on. The two differ only by a padding byte, so their outputs don't match — Keccak-256 is the variant used by Ethereum. Output is hex only.

browser · web crypto
PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA1 · 4096 iterations · 256-bit key — fixed by IEEE 802.11i

Computed locally; nothing is sent anywhere. SHA-1, MD5, and RIPEMD-160 are shown for learning only — MD5 and SHA-1 are broken for collision resistance and unfit for security use. WPA/WPA2 PSK follows IEEE 802.11i: PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA1(passphrase, SSID, 4096, 32).
